                                                                                      salmoides 2 years ago

                                                                                      One lesson I've learned as I've moved to battery powered sensors is to always check your components. My first sensor lasted two weeks instead of several months. It ended up being a "bad" nRF24L01+ that would not power down properly. The defective component would transmit and receive as it should, but would continue to draw about 4 mA after commanded to power down when it should have been drawing a few uA instead. Replacing the transceiver brought the sensor back into proper operation. The moral: Test each component, including the current used, on your breadboard before you turn on your soldering iron.
